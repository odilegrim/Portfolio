Bring Me The Horizon: different music styles

For this plot I have split Bring Me The Horizons music into three different categories, Old Music containing their first four albums (Count Your Blessings, Suicide Season, There is a Hell Believe Me Ive Seen It. There is a Heaven Lets Keep it a Secret and Sempiternal (Expanded Edition)), New music containing their three most recent releases (Thats The Spirit, amo and POST HUMAN: SURVIVAL HORROR) with exception of their experimental music from Music to listen to~, which is the third category. The reason I separated this work from the two main categories, is that it isnt exactly representative of either their new or old music style. Looking at the plot, it is easily visible that Bring Me The Horizons new music is much more varied than their old music. Considering that music with a high energy and low valence is is seen as angry, and high energy combined with high valence as happy, we could conclude that their new music is overall much happier than their old music. The valence is generally higher and the energy overall a bit lower. When looking at the experimental music, we can see that it has very low valence values and most of it leans more toward the angry side, like BMTHs old music. It also overlaps in a category of lower-energy-and-valence-scores with their new music, those scores would mean that the music is more sad. Speaking of that, each category in this plot seems to have outliers in the sad category. For the old music, the track Memorial seems to be extremely sad, A Devastating Liberation seems to be the saddest song from the experimental music and i apologise if you feel something would be the saddest song from their new music.

The danceability is indicated by size and it is immediately visible that especially the sad songs are less danceable than the rest of them. Happier songs, mainly their new music, seem to be the most danceable, but most of the music from Music to listen to~ also seems to be quite danceable. Considering the fact that this album was released in 2019 and is also quite new, we could pose the question whether BMTHs style has changed to a more danceable one. The speechiness, indicated by the colour, generally seems to be higher with higher numbers in valence, but also deffinitely has some outliers. Generally their music doesnt seem to be very speechy. Looking at all their work together, BMTH seems to make very energetic music, with most of their songs having an energy score above 0.75 (maximum score is 1). Their music seems to have progressed from negative and angry towards a more diverse positive and happy style, with almost a kind of dip back towards negativity in their experimental album.
